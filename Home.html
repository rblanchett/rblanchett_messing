Reid Blanchett

Doctoral Candidate, Genetics, Michigan State University

I am a doctoral candidate at Michigan State University in Genetics. My current research explores how genetic variation influences neural circuits and cognitive domains relevant to psychiatric illness, with a focus on infancy and the utilization of X-monosomy as a model for gene dosage effects. I am also heavily involved with mental health advocacy and Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion. My career goal is to work for a philanthropic foundation in the sciences.
